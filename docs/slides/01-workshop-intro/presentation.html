SRv6 in one slide

  • A program in the packet header (RFC 8986)
  • A segment is an instruction, not just a location
  • Linux: seg6 since kernel 4.10 — 2017
  • Ecosystem: FRR, SONiC, Cilium, VPP
  • No new dataplane: it is plain IPv6
Why here. SRv6 is where the network design meets the kernel datapath.

A netdev tradition

0x16
Lisbon, 2022. First SRv6 workshop: the kernel implementation, seg6local behaviors, the early ecosystem.
0x19
Zagreb, 2025. SRv6 goes production: FRR, SONiC, uSID, deployments at scale.
0x1A
Rome, 2026. Three directions that hit the datapath — and the gaps we still have to close.
The thread. Every edition, the interesting work moved closer to the kernel.

Three directions for this edition

1
Source-routed AI backends. ECMP gives no control over the fabric — SRv6 puts path placement in the host and the NIC.
2
L2 services beyond VXLAN. The kernel stops at End.DX2 — multipoint services and EVPN over SRv6 need more.
3
Provider-grade SRv6. End-to-end design on Linux + FRR, observability, and redundancy protection (R-SID).
+
Ecosystem update. SRv6 in Linux (9 years) and in FRR (5 years) of mainline support.
